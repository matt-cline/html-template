Presence of alveolar panels, plasterboard or wood for indoor walls

Addition criterion

Water height > 0.5m

Potential damages

Deformation, swelling, degradation of lightweight partitions and lining consisting of alveolar panels (cardboard base), chipboard panels, gypsum plasterboard.

Vulnerability from the security of people point of view

0 - No impact on personal safety.

Vulnerability from return to normal point of view

3 - When rehabilitation leads to the replacement of all bulkheads and duplicates, this is a cumbersome operation that compromises the reinstatement of the premises for many months. In this case, the vulnerability of the building to the return to normal is strong.

Objectives of prevention works

Prolonged contact with water degrades the qualities of the distribution walls, mostly made up of plaster-based elements (plates, plasters). These degradations are a function of the immersion height, the contact time, the beautiful speed and the pollution of the water carried by the flood.The direct consequences can range from a simple detachment of the wall coverings to the collapse of the partitions. Indirect consequences may relate to electrical or heating installations and equipment integrated into the partition or integral with the partition, as well as the sanitary conditions for the occupancy of the dwelling (retention of organic elements by the materials constituting the partitions). The objective of the measure is essentially to facilitate post-flood restoration work.

Works for the resiting strategy

Works for the yielding strategy

Use presence of bulkheads that can be disassembled for repairs. Avoid alveolar partitions and partitions made of wood-based materials. In addition to their function of delegating and organizing the interior space of the dwelling, the partition walls also ensure the passage or fixing of the equipment will have been damaged or not during the flooding, they will be retained after rehabilitation or replaced. Work may be undertaken either because of flooding or on other occasions, such as when the interior distribution is altered. Cases of rehabilitation after flooding: In the event of destruction of the partitions, the total restoration of the partition is required whatever the type. It is recommended to rebuild partitions on metal framing. In the case of immersion alone, the nature of the repair depends on the type of partition of origin:
- Partition of the type "honeycomb partitions": Total refurbishment is inevitable. It is recommended to rebuild partitions on metal framing.
- Partitions of the type "partition on frame" without mineral wool inside the partition: the conservation of the plasterboard not reached by the water and the capillary ascents is conceivable. The degraded parts of the plates are removed and replaced by new elements which are screwed onto the framework which has been carefully dried beforehand.
- Partitions of the type: "partition on framework with mineral wool inside the partition: it is recommended to replace the mineral wool and to do this to dismantle the plates replacing the deteriorated ones, and reusing the framework which Will have been thoroughly dried beforehand.
- Masonry partitions coated with plaster: the part of the plaster of plaster not reached by the water and the capillary ascents will be preserved. The immersed part will be washed abundantly, dried then possibly redone after harvesting if the hardness has been significantly affected during the flooding.
- Walls made of plaster tile: the wall may be retained if the hardness of the plaster, measured after an abundant wash and a final drying, has not been significantly affected during the flood. Otherwise, the entire partition must be rebuilt. Waterproof plaster tiles or metal frame partitions may be used.
- Wood particle board partitions: the immersed part degrades after a few hours due to swelling, total refurbishment is recommended.
